About me

I am Qianwen Wang (汪倩雯), a PostDoc at Gehlenborgy Lab, Harvard University. I am studying Interactive Data Visualization within the broad context of Human-AI Collaboration, envisioning interactive data visualiation as an effective approach for knowledge communication with general users. My work strives to facilitate the communication between general users and Machine Learning Models through creating interactive visual analysis systems, with a special interest in their applications in solving Biomedical Challenges.

I obtained my PhD degree in the Department of Electronic and Computer Engineering, Hong Kong University of Science and Technology in 2020. I got my B.Eng Degree from Xi'an Jiaotong University in 2015. During my PhD study, I worked at the XAI Group， HKUST VisLab, under the supervision of Prof. Huamin Qu. I have interned at Microsoft Research Asia and collaborate with researchers from MIT, University of Oxford, Tsinghua Univeristy, Microsoft Research.

Research Themes

Human-AI Collaboration

I design and develop tools to facilitate Human-AI collaboration, which also drives my investigation on visualization techniques, algorithms, and design frameworks.

Accessible and Intelligent Visualization

I propose techniques, authoring tools, and machine learning algorithms in pursuit of making visualizations that can be accurately interpreted and easily used by everyone.

VIS+(X)AI in Biomedicine/Healthcare

Through wide collaboration, I am learning how VIS + (X)AI can promote scientific discoveries, especially in the field of biomedicine and healthcare (e.g., genomics, single-cell, and cohort analysis).
